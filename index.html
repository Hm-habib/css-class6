Nilfamari was established as a district in 1984 under Rajshahi division and now functioning under Rangpur division. It has six police stations: Nilfamari, Saidpur, Jaldhaka, Kishorgong, Domar, and Dimla. The historical places of the district include Nilsagar, tomb of Hazrat Pir Mohiuddin (kundupukur), Saidpur airport, Uttra EPZ (Export Processing Zone), Teesta Barrage (Partly shared by Lalmonirhat district), Leprosy Hospital. The district police is working to maintain law and order and establish peace and social tranquility Nilphamari, a picturesque district in northern Bangladesh, is a captivating blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. Situated along the banks of the majestic Tista River, it offers serene landscapes, fertile plains, and historical landmarks. The district is renowned for its agricultural abundance, particularly rice cultivation, which contributes significantly to the nation's food security. Beyond its agricultural prowess, Nilphamari boasts diverse attractions, including the serene Nil Sagar Lake, the historic Chini Mosque, and the vibrant city of Saidpur. The district's strategic location and well-developed infrastructure make it a vital transportation hub. The Saidpur Airport serves as a gateway to the region, facilitating domestic and international connectivity. With its rich cultural tapestry, natural wonders, and growing economy, Nilphamari continues to be a significant contributor to Bangladesh's development.
