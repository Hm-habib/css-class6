Panchagarh, a serene district nestled in the northernmost part of Bangladesh, offers a captivating blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. Known for its lush tea gardens, picturesque landscapes, and rich history, Panchagarh attracts visitors with its unique charm.   The district's proximity to the Himalayas influences its climate and topography, creating a diverse ecosystem. The Tetulia Tea Garden, one of the oldest in Bangladesh, sprawls across rolling hills, offering breathtaking views and a glimpse into the tea-making process.   Panchagarh also boasts historical sites, including ancient temples and mosques, reflecting the region's rich cultural heritage. The district's indigenous communities, such as the Rajbanshi and the Oraon, add to its cultural diversity.
